## Licensing Principles

These principles capture the intended commercial posture for the AntiVirus project as it approaches production readiness.

### Endpoint protection availability

- The endpoint protection product is intended to be free to download and use.
- Core endpoint protection capabilities should remain available without paywalls, feature caps, reseller tiers, or time-limited trials.

### Sharing and redistribution

- The software may be shared at no cost.
- Redistribution must not be used as the basis for charging other users for access to the endpoint protection product itself.

### Reseller restriction

- No reseller or partner licensing model should permit charging third parties for the endpoint protection software.
- Managed use, bundling, or redistribution that introduces paid resale of the antivirus product should be prohibited in the production license.

### Product direction

- The product goal is to provide a credible, modern, publicly available antivirus offering with enterprise-style protection capabilities.
- Monetization, if ever introduced, should not weaken the free availability of core endpoint protection.

### Production follow-up

Before release, these principles should be converted into:

- formal license text
- installer and product EULA language
- website distribution terms
- support and acceptable-use policy wording
